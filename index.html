Data Science for Social Good Summer Fellowship 2013

Want to use your data mining skills for social good? Love the intellectual rigor of science, but want to work on real-world problems? Looking for a meaningful bridge into the growing field of data science?

The Computation Institute at the University of Chicago and Argonne National Laboratory invite you to apply for the 2013 Data Science for Social Good summer fellowship.

The program

We’re training future data scientists to work on the world’s most challenging social problems.

Fellows will work in small teams with mentors from the Obama campaign analytics team and seasoned data scientists from academia and business on high-impact projects in education, healthcare, energy, transportation, and more.


This full-time program is selective, intensive, and hands-on:

- You’ll work with nonprofits and governments to solve big problems with data.

- You’ll learn how to apply statistics, machine learning, and big data technologies to problems that matter.

- And you’ll work collaboratively with interdisciplinary teams.


The fellowship stipend is competitive and based on your experience. We’ll house you in Chicago for the summer, from early June to late August. (Exact dates coming soon.)

Our advisors include Eric Schmidt (Google), Rayid Ghani (former Chief Scientist for Obama 2012 campaign), and Jake Porway (DataKind).

Who we're looking for

We're looking for PhD, masters, and advanced undergraduate students in the computer science, statistics, and the computational and quantitative sciences. If you're an amazing software engineer with a serious interest in data science, we want to hear from you too.

You’ll need statistics, programming, and data skills - but you don’t need to be an expert in every area.

Most of all, we want people who are passionate about using data for social impact.

Frequently asked questions

Here are the questions we hear most often about the program.


Are you also looking for mentors and projects for the program?

Yes. Mentors must have experience with applied data science projects. You’ll help scope projects, lead a team, and mentor fellows.

If you’re qualified, contact us - we have full-time, paid mentor positions available. We can always use more volunteer mentors as well.


Can I do the fellowship in the evening or part-time?

No. We expect fellows to work on these projects full-time during the summer.


Do I physically need to be in Chicago?

Yes.


I’m on an F-1/J-1 visa. Can I apply?

Yes. If you’re authorized to work and get paid by us, you’re eligible to apply.


I’m not a US citizen or permanent resident. Can I apply?

If you’re able to get a visa (by yourself, without sponsorship from us) that allows you to work in the US for the summer - and get paid by us - you can apply.
